```{exercise} 16
:label: infst-ex2-sol
:nonumber:

(a)
&emsp;(i) Judgemental sampling would allow the manager to pick out students who have participated in lots of enrichment activities, or those who would benefit most from them, which might mean the data is richer and more useful for decision-making based on opinions. However, it would not be a reflective sample of the whole cohort, and might be biased in favour of students on certain courses.

&emsp;(ii) Proportional stratified sampling would give a balance of students from across the courses, and give a more representative sample. However, many of the students chosen randomly might not have much experience of enrichment activities, and you can't guarantee that everyone chosen will complete the survey (using the budget to provide incentives for participation might help). This would be more useful for quantitative data about the proportion of students participating in enrichment.

(b) The student data could be filtered by course and split out into four separate lists. For each course, you would multiply the number of students taking that course by 100 and divide by 1000 to get the number of students from that course in a representative sample, which would be 42, 34, 20 and 4 (rounding to the nearest student) making up the sample of 100 students. Then, this many random numbers will be chosen, using a random number function like `=RANDBETWEEN(1, 420)` filled across 42 cells, to pick this number of students from each cohort to make up the sample.

```
