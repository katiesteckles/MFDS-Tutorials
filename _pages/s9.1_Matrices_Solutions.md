```{exercise}
\begin{eqnarray*} \operatorname{det}A &=& 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 4 & 5 \end{vmatrix} - 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 6 & 2 \\ 2 & 5 \end{vmatrix} + 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 6 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 \end{vmatrix} \\

        \end{eqnarray*}
         
&emsp; (b) $A' = \begin{pmatrix}  2 & 4 & 5\\ 6 & 1 & 2\\ 1 & 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$

Expand along the bottom row. Then
\begin{eqnarray*} \operatorname{det}A &=& 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 4 & 5 \\ 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 5 \\ 6 & 2 \end{vmatrix} + 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 6 & 1 \end{vmatrix} \\
        &=& 1 \cdot (4 \cdot 2- 1\cdot 5) - 1 \cdot(2\cdot 2 - 6 \cdot 5) + 3 \cdot (2 \cdot 1 - 6 \cdot 4) \\
         &=& 3 - (-26) + (-66)\\
         &=& -37
        \end{eqnarray*}
         
&emsp; (c) $B = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 3\\  1 & 1 & 3\\ 1 & 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$

Expand along the bottom row. Then
\begin{eqnarray*} \operatorname{det}A &=& 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 1 & 3 \end{vmatrix} - 1 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 1 & 3 \end{vmatrix} + 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{vmatrix} \\
        &=& 1 \cdot (1 \cdot 3- 3\cdot 1) - 1 \cdot(1\cdot 3 - 3 \cdot 1) + 3 \cdot (1 \cdot 1 - 1 \cdot 1) \\
         &=& 0 - 0 + 0\\
         &=& 0
        \end{eqnarray*}
```

```{exercise}
:label: mats-ex2-sol

&emsp;(a) Expand along the first row.
\begin{eqnarray*} \operatorname{det}C &=& 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ -1 & 5 \end{vmatrix} - (-1) \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 5 \end{vmatrix} + 4 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 5 \\ -2 & -1 \end{vmatrix} \\
        &=& 3 \cdot (5 \cdot 5- 2\cdot (-1)) + 1 \cdot(1\cdot 5 - 2 \cdot (-2)) + 4 \cdot (1 \cdot (-1) - 5 \cdot (-2)) \\
         &=& 81 + 9 + 36\\
         &=& 126
        \end{eqnarray*}
        
&emsp;(b) $C^{\mathsf{T}} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 1 & -2\\
     -1 & 5 & -1\\
    4 & 2 & 5
     \end{pmatrix}$

$\operatorname{det}C^{\mathsf{T}} = \operatorname{det}C$.
        
Indeed, expand down the first column:
\begin{eqnarray*} \operatorname{det}C &=& 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 5 & -1 \\ 2 & 5 \end{vmatrix} - (-1) \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 2 & 5 \end{vmatrix} + 4 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 5 & -1 \end{vmatrix} \\
        &=& 3 \cdot (5 \cdot 5 - (-1)\cdot 2) + 1 \cdot(1\cdot 5 - (-2) \cdot 2) + 4 \cdot (1 \cdot (-1) - (-2) \cdot 5) \\
         &=& 81 + 9 + 36\\
         &=& 126
        \end{eqnarray*}
&emsp;(c)

$C + \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\
     0 & 0 & 0\\
    2 & 1 & -5
     \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 & 4\\
     1 & 5 & 2\\
    0 & 0 & 0
     \end{pmatrix}$
     
Expand along the bottom row, and the terms will all be multiplied by zero, so the determinant is zero.
     
&emsp;(d) $C - \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0\\
     0 & 0 & 0\\
    2 & 1 & -5
     \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 & 4\\
     1 & 5 & 2\\
    -4& -2& -10
     \end{pmatrix} $
     
Expand along the first row:
\begin{eqnarray*} && 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ -2 & 10 \end{vmatrix} - (-1) \cdot \begin{vmatrix} -1 & 4 \\ -4 & 10 \end{vmatrix} + 4 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} -1 & 5\\ -4 & -2\end{vmatrix} \\
        &=& 3 \cdot (5 \cdot 10 - 2\cdot (-2)) - 1 \cdot ((-1) \cdot 10 - 4\cdot (-4)) + 8 \cdot ((-1) \cdot (-2) - 5\cdot (-4)) \\
         &=& 162 - (-18) + 72\\
         &=& 252
        \end{eqnarray*}
        

&emsp;(e) $C - \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 & 4\\
     1 & 0 & 2\\
    -2 & -1 & 0
     \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 0 & 0\\
     0 & 5 & 0\\
    0 & 0 & 5
     \end{pmatrix}$

Expand along the first row:
\begin{eqnarray*} && 3 \cdot \begin{vmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 5 \end{vmatrix}\\
        &=& 3 \cdot (5 \cdot 5- 0\cdot0) \\
         &=& 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 5\\
         &=& 75
        \end{eqnarray*}
In general, the determinant of a diagonal matrix (one whose off-diagonal entries are all zero) is the product of the entries on the diagonal.
```
