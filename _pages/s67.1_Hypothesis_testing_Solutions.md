
# Hypothesis Testing


```{exercise}
:label: hypte-ex1-sol

&emsp; (a) 
$H_0$ : $\mu = 170$ minutes
$H_1$ : \mu \neq 170$ minutes

Use a two-tailed t-test with 5% significance. The test statistic is calculated as $t = 1.53$, and the critical region is less than $−1.96$ OR more than $1.96$. Since the critical value is outside this region, we do not reject the null hypothesis. There is insufficient evidence to suggest the mean time spent by students in the dojo is different from 170 minutes.

&emsp;(b) With this new data, the test statistic is $1.87$. This is still outside the critical region, so the result is still not significant.

```

Degrees of freedom: $\nu = (2-1)(2-1) = 1$

Then $\chi^2 = 3.506+3.391+2.773+2.683 = 12.353$

For 5% significance, the critical value is $3.841$. As $12.353 > 3.841$ there is evidence of association between the type of drug taken and improvement. We reject the null hypothesis.
